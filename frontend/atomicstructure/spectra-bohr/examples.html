Q: Calculate the radius of the 3rd orbit (n=3) for a He‚Å∫ ion (Z=2). ($a_0 = 0.529$ √Ö)

‚úÖ Solution:
Use Bohr radius formula: $r_n = \frac{n^2}{Z} \times a_0$

‚≠ê Example 2: Energy Level Calculation

Q: Calculate the energy (in eV) of an electron in the n=2 state of a Li¬≤‚Å∫ ion (Z=3). ($E_0 = 13.6$ eV)

‚úÖ Solution:
Use Bohr energy formula: $E_n = -E_0 \frac{Z^2}{n^2}$
$E_2 = -13.6 \text{ eV} \times \frac{3^2}{2^2} = -13.6 \text{ eV} \times \frac{9}{4}$
$E_2 = -13.6 \times 2.25 \text{ eV} = \mathbf{-30.6 \text{ eV}}$

‚≠ê Example 3: Spectral Line Wavelength (Rydberg)

Q: Find the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the photon emitted when an electron in a Hydrogen atom (Z=1) transitions from n=3 to n=2 (Balmer series). ($R_H = 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}$)

‚úÖ Solution:
Use Rydberg formula: $\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H Z^2 \left( \frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2} \right)$
Here $n_1=2$, $n_2=3$, $Z=1$.
$\frac{1}{\lambda} = (1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}) (1^2) \left( \frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right)$
$\frac{1}{\lambda} = (1.097 \times 10^7) \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{9} \right) = (1.097 \times 10^7) \left( \frac{9-4}{36} \right)$
$\frac{1}{\lambda} = (1.097 \times 10^7) \left( \frac{5}{36} \right) \approx 1.5236 \times 10^6 \text{ m}^{-1}$
$\lambda = \frac{1}{1.5236 \times 10^6} \approx 6.56 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m} = \mathbf{656 \text{ nm}}$ (This is the red H-alpha line!)
