Example Problems: Nature of Roots

Practice predicting root types using the discriminant!

⭐ Example 1 (D > 0)

Q: Determine the nature of the roots for $2x^2 - 7x + 3 = 0$.

✅ Solution:
$a=2, b=-7, c=3$.
$D = b^2 - 4ac = (-7)^2 - 4(2)(3) = 49 - 24 = 25$.
Since $D = 25 > 0$, the roots are **real and distinct**.

⭐ Example 2 (D = 0)

Q: Determine the nature of the roots for $x^2 - 10x + 25 = 0$.

✅ Solution:
$a=1, b=-10, c=25$.
$D = b^2 - 4ac = (-10)^2 - 4(1)(25) = 100 - 100 = 0$.
Since $D = 0$, the roots are **real and equal**.

⭐ Example 3 (D < 0)

Q: Determine the nature of the roots for $x^2 + x + 1 = 0$.

✅ Solution:
$a=1, b=1, c=1$.
$D = b^2 - 4ac = (1)^2 - 4(1)(1) = 1 - 4 = -3$.
Since $D = -3 < 0$, the roots are **complex conjugates** (not real).
