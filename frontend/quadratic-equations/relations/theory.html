üß© Level 1 Concept ‚Äì Defining the Roots ($\alpha, \beta$)

For a standard quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ (where $a \neq 0$), the solutions or roots are the values of $x$ that satisfy the equation. Let's denote these roots as $\alpha$ and $\beta$.

These roots can be found using the quadratic formula: $\alpha, \beta = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$.

‚öîÔ∏è Level 2 Concept ‚Äì Vieta's Formula: Sum of Roots

Without actually calculating the individual roots, we can find their sum directly from the coefficients.

The **sum of the roots** is given by: $\mathbf{\alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a}}$

Just take the negative of the middle coefficient ($b$) and divide by the leading coefficient ($a$). Easy peasy!

üßó Level 3 Concept ‚Äì Vieta's Formula: Product of Roots

Similarly, we can find the product of the roots directly from the coefficients.

The **product of the roots** is given by: $\mathbf{\alpha \beta = \frac{c}{a}}$

This time, take the constant term ($c$) and divide by the leading coefficient ($a$). Vieta makes calculations faster!

üßô‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è Level 4 Concept ‚Äì Forming a Quadratic Equation from Roots

If you know the roots $\alpha$ and $\beta$, you can construct the quadratic equation.

A quadratic equation with roots $\alpha$ and $\beta$ can be written as: $k(x - \alpha)(x - \beta) = 0$, where $k$ is any non-zero constant.

Expanding this (and usually taking $k=1$ for simplicity) gives the useful form:

$\mathbf{x^2 - (\alpha + \beta)x + (\alpha \beta) = 0}$

Or, $\mathbf{x^2 - (\text{Sum of Roots})x + (\text{Product of Roots}) = 0}$
