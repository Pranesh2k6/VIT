Example Problems: Discriminant

Practice calculating $D = b^2 - 4ac$!

⭐ Example 1

Q: Find the discriminant of $x^2 + 6x + 5 = 0$.

✅ Solution:
Here, $a=1$, $b=6$, $c=5$.
$D = b^2 - 4ac = (6)^2 - 4(1)(5) = 36 - 20 = \mathbf{16}$.

⭐ Example 2

Q: Calculate the discriminant for $3x^2 - 2x + 1 = 0$.

✅ Solution:
Here, $a=3$, $b=-2$, $c=1$.
$D = b^2 - 4ac = (-2)^2 - 4(3)(1) = 4 - 12 = \mathbf{-8}$.

⭐ Example 3

Q: What is the discriminant of $4x^2 + 12x + 9 = 0$?

✅ Solution:
Here, $a=4$, $b=12$, $c=9$.
$D = b^2 - 4ac = (12)^2 - 4(4)(9) = 144 - 144 = \mathbf{0}$.
