üß© Concept 1: Electromagnetic Spectrum

Electromagnetic radiation is energy that travels in waves. It includes radio waves, microwaves, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-rays, and gamma rays, ordered by increasing frequency ($\nu$) and energy ($E$), and decreasing wavelength ($\lambda$). Key relationships are $c = \lambda\nu$ (where $c$ is the speed of light) and $E = h\nu = hc/\lambda$ (where $h$ is Planck's constant).

‚öîÔ∏è Concept 2: Bohr's Model Limitations (Review)

While a crucial step, Bohr's model of fixed orbits only worked perfectly for Hydrogen and other single-electron species (like He‚Å∫). It failed to explain:

  • The spectra of multi-electron atoms.
  • The splitting of spectral lines into finer lines (fine structure).
  • The splitting of spectral lines in magnetic fields (Zeeman effect) or electric fields (Stark effect).
  • Why electrons in certain orbits were stable (didn't incorporate wave nature or uncertainty principle).

Bohr gave us a good map of the hydrogen solar system, but the rest of the atomic galaxy needed quantum mechanics!

üßó Concept 3: Calculations using Bohr's Model

Despite its limits, Bohr's formulas are essential for JEE calculations involving **Hydrogen-like species** (single electron, nucleus with charge $+Ze$):

  • Radius of n-th orbit: $r_n = \frac{n^2}{Z} \times a_0$, where $a_0 \approx 0.529$ √Ö (Bohr radius).
  • Energy of n-th orbit: $E_n = -E_0 \frac{Z^2}{n^2}$, where $E_0 \approx 13.6$ eV (Ionization energy of H).
  • Velocity of electron in n-th orbit: $v_n = v_0 \frac{Z}{n}$, where $v_0 \approx 2.18 \times 10^6$ m/s.
  • Spectral Lines (Rydberg Formula): For a transition from $n_2 \rightarrow n_1$ (where $n_2 > n_1$), the emitted photon's wavenumber ($\bar{\nu} = 1/\lambda$) is given by $\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H Z^2 \left( \frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2} \right)$, where $R_H \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}$ is the Rydberg constant.

Memorize these formulas, Cadet! They are your tools for calculating energy jumps and light emissions in simple atoms.
