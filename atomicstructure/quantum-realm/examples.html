Example Problems: Quantum Realm

Practice with Planck, Bohr, and de Broglie!

⭐ Example 1: Planck's Equation

Q: Calculate the energy (in Joules) of a photon of green light with a frequency ($\nu$) of $6.0 \times 10^{14}$ Hz. (Planck's constant $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s)

✅ Solution:
Use Planck's equation: $E = h\nu$
$E = (6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J·s}) \times (6.0 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz})$
$E = 3.9756 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J} \approx \mathbf{3.98 \times 10^{-19} J}$

⭐ Example 2: Bohr Model Energy Levels

Q: What is the energy (in eV) required to excite an electron in a hydrogen atom from the ground state (n=1) to the first excited state (n=2)? (Energy of ground state = -13.6 eV)

✅ Solution:
Energy formula: $E_n = -13.6 \frac{Z^2}{n^2}$ eV. For Hydrogen, Z=1.
$E_1 = -13.6 \frac{1^2}{1^2} = -13.6$ eV
$E_2 = -13.6 \frac{1^2}{2^2} = -13.6 / 4 = -3.4$ eV
Energy required = $\Delta E = E_{final} - E_{initial} = E_2 - E_1$
$\Delta E = (-3.4 \text{ eV}) - (-13.6 \text{ eV}) = \mathbf{10.2 \text{ eV}}$

⭐ Example 3: de Broglie Wavelength

Q: Calculate the de Broglie wavelength ($\lambda$) of an electron (mass $m_e = 9.1 \times 10^{-31}$ kg) moving at a velocity ($v$) of $1.0 \times 10^6$ m/s. ($h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s)

✅ Solution:
Use de Broglie's equation: $\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$
$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J·s}}{(9.1 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}) \times (1.0 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s})}$
$\lambda \approx 7.28 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}$ (or 0.728 nm or 7.28 Å)
