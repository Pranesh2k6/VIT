Atoms aren't indivisible! Key components were discovered:

  • Electron: Found using Cathode Ray Tubes, has a negative charge (-e).
  • Proton: Found in the nucleus, has a positive charge (+e).
  • Neutron: Also in the nucleus, has no charge (neutral).

Think of them like building blocks: protons and neutrons give mass, electrons buzz around!

J.J. Thomson imagined the atom as a "Plum Pudding" - a sphere of positive 'pudding' with negative electrons ('plums') scattered inside.

Strength: Explained overall neutrality.

Weakness: Couldn't explain results of later experiments, like alpha scattering.
