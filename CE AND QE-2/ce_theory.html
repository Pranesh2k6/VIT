🎯 The Complex Quest II: The Modulus–Argument Saga

Ah, Cadet! Ready to unlock the secrets of the Imaginary Realm? Let’s power up your JEE XP!

🏝️ LEVEL 1: Enter the Modulus Realm

A complex number is $z = a + ib$. It can also be written as $z = r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)$. This is the **Modulus–Argument Form (Polar Form)**.

SymbolMeaning
$r$**Modulus** = distance from origin $\rightarrow r=\sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$
$\theta$**Argument** = angle with positive x-axis $\rightarrow \theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{b}{a}\right)$

💡 Think of $r$ as power, and $\theta$ as aim of your spell!

⚙️ LEVEL 2: Locating the Hero (Finding Quadrants)

When finding $\theta$, remember which quadrant you are in:

QuadrantSign of $(a, b)$$\theta$ rangeExample
I$(+, +)$$0^\circ < \theta < 90^\circ$$1+i$
II$(-, +)$$90^\circ < \theta < 180^\circ$$-1+i$
III$(-, -)$$180^\circ < \theta < 270^\circ$$-1-i$
IV$(+, -)$$270^\circ < \theta < 360^\circ$$1-i$

💬 **Sage’s Tip**: Always adjust $\theta$ according to the signs of $a$ and $b$ — don’t rely only on $\tan^{-1}$!

🧭 LEVEL 3: The Polar Portal Opens

You’ve unlocked the Modulus and Argument. Now, express your number in full magical form!

🔮 **Polar Representation**: $z=r(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta)$

Or using **Euler’s Form**: $z=re^{i\theta}$, where $e^{i\theta}=\cos\theta+i\sin\theta$.

💬 **Power-up Move**:
Multiplying two complex numbers $\rightarrow$ multiply their moduli, add their arguments.
Dividing two $\rightarrow$ divide their moduli, subtract their arguments.

⚡ LEVEL 4: The Argument Trap

For a number $z=a+ib$, its argument can be represented as $\arg(z)=\Theta+2n\pi, n\in\mathbb{Z}$.

✅ **Principal Argument $(\operatorname{Arg} z)$**: The unique value of $\theta$ between $(-\pi, \pi]$ (or $-180^\circ, 180^\circ]$).
